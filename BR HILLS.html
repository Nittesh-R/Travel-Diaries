Introduction
    Welcome :
  • BR Hills, located in South Eastern Karnataka, is a unique wildlife sanctuary at the intersection of the Western and Eastern Ghats. Named after the Rangaswamy Temple on a white cliff, it’s a popular pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Ranganatha.The sanctuary features diverse vegetation and is home to various animals like gaurs, bears, tigers, elephants, and over 200 bird species. Adventure seekers can enjoy trekking, rafting, fishing, and boat riding on the Kapila and Cauvery rivers.The best time to visit is from June to October, when the rains enhance the wildlife experience. Whether for spiritual or adventure purposes, BR Hills is an ideal destination.
Destination
    Overview
  • BR Hills, also known as Biligiriranga Hills, is a scenic destination in Karnataka where the Western Ghats merge with the Eastern Ghats. It's famous for the Biligiri Rangaswamy Temple, offering stunning views and cultural importance. The BRT Wildlife Sanctuary is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts, home to elephants, tigers, and various birds. With trekking trails through lush forests, BR Hills is an ideal spot for nature lovers seeking peace and diverse wildlife in Karnataka's beautiful surroundings.

Though it was undergoing renovations, the temple was still vibrant with rituals honoring deities like Biligiri Ranganatha and Alumelamma (Lakshmi). We were fortunate to witness a special pooja dedicated to Lord Hanuman, known as "Apura Gopura," which was a deeply moving experience.
      • From the temple, we enjoyed sweeping views of green hills and tiny villages below—a scene that felt straight out of a painting. It was incredibly peaceful and refreshing, surrounded by such natural beauty.

    • Travel Tips :

      Best time to visit:
      Plan your trip between October and February to enjoy pleasant weather and lush greenery.
    • Clothing and gear pack:
      Pack light, comfortable clothing suitable for trekking, avoid more plastic covers,carry sanitizer or vasline to get rid of leach, Sturdy footwear is essential for exploring the hilly terrain.
    • Accommodation:
      Book accommodations in advance, especially during peak seasons, to ensure availability. Consider staying in local lodges or resorts for an immersive experience.
    • Wildlife Etiquette:
      Respect wildlife and maintain a safe distance when encountering animals. Follow sanctuary guidelines to preserve the natural habitat.
    • local cuisine:
      Explore local eateries to savor authentic Karnataka cuisine. Don’t miss trying traditional dishes that reflect the region’s cultural heritage.
